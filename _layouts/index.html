About Me

Hello, I am Anton Lundh , a 21 year old engineer from Sweden .
I am passioned about technology with a sweetspot for 3D printing, mechatronics and cyber security!

My fasination for 3D Printers started back in 2017 with the purchase of my first 3D Printer, a Tevo Tarantula. A simple printer kit from China that I upgraded for far more than the original cost of the kit. In the end I redesigned it from the ground up back in 2019/2020, so it now features two independently controlable extruders. Allowing for printing two of the same parts at the same time, and multicolor printing. My second 3D Printer was a Prusa 3D MK3 a printer that I still to this day use. It was great from the start, but I still decided to upgrade it with a more rigid frame and a better extruder.

My love for mechatronics came almost naturally after years of watching cool project being built by others. I decided to join the action, by building others cool projects and starting designing my own. My goto kind of project is some kind of home automation, making my life in some way easier (usually ). Some of my favorites that are designed by me can be found under the “Projects” tab.

My interest in cyber security, came sometime between 2016 and 2018, after stumbling upon the great site haveibeenpwned.com by Troy Hunt and finding out some of my emails had appered in some data breaches. Since then it has flourished and I am now intrested in keeping my self secure with one of the highest tires of two factor authentication and longer randomized passwords. No need to mention details, because one of the strongest methods of security is after all obscurity ! I also try to keep my home network safe by runing pfSense with multiple segmented VLANs to minimize the attack vector. In the end this has led me to chosing to orient my future career and masters degree towards cyber security.

All of my interests combined lead my to deciding to be a part of the board for the student association Lindholmens Makerspace during my thrid year at Chalmers, offering help and a space for other students to build and design at Chalmers. During my time at Lindholmens Makerspace I was responisble for taking notes at our meetings, our 3D Printers, designing the posters we put up around Chalmers advertising our events, knowing what we had in stock and where it was located, and keeping our tools and electronics stock up to date with what the members wanted.
